Export of vacuum equipment

The IVC Plasmainstrument company offers the service of exporting vacuum equipment.

When exporting equipment, the exporter faces a number of issues:

-        Specifics of tax legislation for export (desk tax audit).

-        Bank transfers from abroad. It is no secret that due to the difficult political situation, payments do not always reach Russian banks.

-        Currency contract. A correctly drawn up foreign exchange contract allows you to avoid problems not only with Russian customs and tax authorities, but also with foreign counterparties.

-        Product certification issues. Which countries require certification, types of certification and what are the financial and time costs. To which countries is it possible to supply equipment without certification? Options for supplying equipment without certification.

-        FSTEC examination. Vacuum equipment is used to produce dual-use products. Therefore, a conclusion from FSTEC on permission to export equipment is often required. It is better to take care of this issue at the stage of signing the contract, and not when the cargo has arrived at customs.

-        Insurance, logistics and customs clearance. What kind of transport is best to transport, what risks to insure, packaging requirements (for example: the exporter had his cargo stuck at foreign customs due to the lack of phytosanitary treatment for wooden containers. Questions of customs declaration.

-        Payment procedure for equipment. The equipment manufacturer ships the equipment with 100% advance payment, and the buyer abroad pays only for 100% delivery.

Advantages of working with IVC Plasmainstrument as a center for the export of vacuum equipment:

-        Our company enters into an agreement with a Russian manufacturer of vacuum equipment, and enters into an agreement with a foreign customer. We handle issues with foreign exchange contracts, interaction with Russian and foreign customs, delivery, insurance and logistics.

-        Professional technical translation of documentation and contracts.

-        Attracting government measures to support exports.

-        Promotion of products through foreign partners.         

-        Participation in foreign specialized exhibitions.

-        Successful experience in exporting vacuum equipment.

There is also an English-speaking staff of engineering and technical workers involved in commissioning of equipment, development of the technical process, carrying out acceptance tests, and training of customer personnel.

Requirements for exported vacuum equipment:

Equipment type:

-        Vacuum coating installations (magnetron, arc, thermal, electron beam, ion beam methods, molecular layer deposition (ALD) methods).

-        Vacuum pumps and fittings.

-        Vacuum ovens.

-        Vacuum nitriding and carburizing plants.

-        Technological sources and power supplies, other components.

-        Consumables, lubricants, oils and targets.

The equipment must be mass-produced for at least three years. Additionally, the supply of spare parts and accessories for five years after delivery of the equipment.

Availability of a set of operational documents.
