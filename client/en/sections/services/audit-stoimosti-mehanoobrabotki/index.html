Machining cost audit

Mechanical processing outsourcing audit is an analytical event aimed at a comprehensive analysis of the efficiency of using enterprise funds for the production of outsourced parts.

Very often, when placing parts on the side, an enterprise orders parts at “average” market prices. Often the “average” price is the maximum price (and sometimes several times higher) accepted on the market. This happens due to various reasons: poor technological development, small coverage of contractors, a corruption component, and banal laziness of the performers.

Machining outsourcing audit allows you to obtain an independent assessment of the cost of manufacturing parts

Who are our clients:

  • Business owners;
  • Outsourced parts manufacturing service;
  • Companies organizing tenders for outsourcing machining, tenders for the supply of manufacturing spare parts for equipment;
  • Companies participating in tenders;
  • Companies organizing the production of products, including serial ones.

Our services:

  • Simplified assessment of the cost of manufacturing parts (Express assessment);
  • Extended estimate of the cost of manufacturing parts (Extended estimate);
  • Audit is a full analysis of the full production cycle of a part, including design analysis, selection of materials, tolerances, manufacturing methods, logistics services, production of equipment, documentation, contract terms and other factors affecting the final cost of parts. (Machining audit);

What our services provide:

  • An express assessment (of the cost of manufacturing parts) will allow you to detect and close “holes” in the company through which money flows out of the company. Which will ultimately increase the company’s profit and the profitability of the project.
  • An expanded estimate will allow you to negotiate reasonably with your current machining manufacturer regarding the cost of manufacturing parts.
  • The audit report will allow you to get a full analysis of the cost of the product manufacturing cycle, as well as key steps to reduce the cost of the product.

Our task, by increasing the manufacturability of the product, is to improve product quality, reduce costs, increase the yield of suitable products, and ensure timely delivery of parts to the warehouse.

Example project:

A company that manufactures components for dairy plants ordered an audit of the cost of machining an adapter made of stainless steel. Previously, the technology involved turning a part from a circle with a diameter of 150 mm. Moreover, more than 70% of the weight went into chips. Our solution, one of which was the use of a thick-walled pipe as a blank, allowed us to reduce the cost of the part, reduce the production time, and free up the working time of the machine. Thus, the company saved 270,000 rubles in the first quarter of implementing the solution and refused to purchase a new machine.

Example project:

A company that manufactures attachments for motor vehicles ordered an extended estimate for serial standard parts manufactured outsourced. Thanks to the expanded estimate, the customer reasonably reduced the cost of manufacturing standard parts by 15%.
